Enigma Machine

Primary Source:

The creation of Enigma from the German in ww2 made a significant advancement to applied mathematics. Enigma used mathematics to encrypt german messages, as the machine had 158,962,555,217,826,360,000 different combinations to it, making it nearly impossible to crack. The motive of the creation of enigma stemmed from the need of having a safe reliable way of communication to their army, and co-ordinate attacks. Enigma uses a modern electric current to pass through different scrambling elements and then display the ciphered letter of the lamp board. Enigma revolutionized codes, as it allowed for many future ways of sending encrypted messages to others, the British even created a better enigma Typex, which was basically the same as the enigma but without the fatal flaws. The primary enigma source shows the mechanical gears of the enigma and shows how mathematics can be used to create an encrypted message.
